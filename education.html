Senior Capstone Project
"P-DAQ"

View on GitHub

For my 2024 senior capstone project I worked as part of a team on a project we called the "personal data acquisition prototype", or "P-DAQ". The team consisted of myself, my classmates Aidan Agee, Jake Goodwin, and Patrick Iacob, along with an industry professional project partner Chris Patton. Our project was a prototype data logger of the likes found in the aerospace and automotive industries, but designed for the budget and use cases of amatuer hobbyists.

The project was developed from start to finished in Rust over nine months with the direction of our project partner Chris Patton and the oversight of a teacher's assistant. After completion, me and my teammates presented our project at a public expo for college of engineering captone projects.

User Interface

View on GitHub

We split our team into two subteams based on expertise to focus on different aspects of the project: a hardware and firmware subteam, and a software subteam. Myself and Aidan Agee were on the software subteam, and we focused on the user interface and data storage.

The user interface was designed with the eGui Rust framework, and hosted as a Rust Rocket webserver from the Raspberry PI, which could then be connected to over WiFi by a user's personal device.
